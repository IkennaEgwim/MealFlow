Jollof Rice Recipe

Ingredients:
  1. Rice
  2. Coconut oil or Vegetable oil
  3. Carrot
  4. Onion
  5. Green beans
  6. Salt
  7. Bell pepper
  8. Sweet corn
  9. Curry powder
  10. Thyme
  11. Fresh black pepper
  12. Dried bay leaves
Instructions:
    Prepare the Ingredients:
  1. Rinse rice thoroughly and drain.
  2. Chop onion, bell pepper, carrot, and green beans.
  3. Drain sweet corn and set aside.
  4. Crush dried bay leaves for flavor.
    5. Sauté Vegetables:
  5. Heat oil in a pot, sauté onions until translucent.
  6. Add bell pepper, carrot, and green beans, cook until soft.
    7. Seasoning:
  7. Add curry powder, thyme, black pepper, and bay leaves.
  8. Season with salt, stir to combine.
    9. Add Rice and Liquid:
  9. Stir rice into the pot until coated.
  10. Add enough liquid to cover rice (1.5 to 2 cups per cup of rice).
    11. Cooking:
  11. Bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 20-25 minutes until rice is tender.
  12. Check and add more liquid if needed.
    13. Final Touches:
  13. Fluff rice with a fork, mix in sweet corn.
  14. Adjust seasoning if necessary.
    15. Serving:
  15. Serve hot as a main or side dish with protein.
  16. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Egusi Soup and Poundo Recipe

Ingredients:
  1. Egusi (melon seeds)
  2. Vgetables (spinach, bitter leaf, or pumpkin leaves)
  3. Onion
  4. Tomatoes
  5. Palm oil
  6. Crayfish
  7. Stock cubes or seasoning powder
  8. Salt
  9. Water, as needed
  10. Cassava or plantain flour
  11. Salt, to taste
Instructions:
    Instructions:
  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
  2. Rinse the egusi seeds thoroughly and drain.
  3. Chop the vegetables (spinach, bitter leaf, or pumpkin leaves).
  4. Chop the onion and tomatoes.
  5. Grind or blend the crayfish into a fine powder.
  6. Cook the Egusi Soup:
  7. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat.
  8. Sauté chopped onions until translucent.
  9. Add ground egusi powder and cook until it bubbles and releases aroma.
  10. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until softened.
  11. Pour in water gradually, stirring continuously until desired consistency.
  12. Season with stock cubes or seasoning powder, salt, and crayfish powder. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  13. Add chopped vegetables and simmer until cooked but still vibrant.
  14. Prepare the Poundo:
  15. Bring water to a boil in a separate pot.
  16. Gradually add cassava or plantain flour, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  17. Stir until mixture thickens and forms a smooth, stretchy dough-like consistency.
  18. Season with salt to taste.
  19. Serve:
  20. Serve the hot egusi soup alongside the poundo in separate bowls.
  21. Enjoy your delicious and nutritious meal!
